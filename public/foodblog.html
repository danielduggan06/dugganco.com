Where the site started

Before the homepage redesigns and consulting pages, this was the part of the site that actually felt alive. It was simple: cook something, take a picture, save the link, and leave a note with a little personality instead of pretending the internet always needs to be polished.

Featured memory

Chicken adobo and a little diplomacy

Some of these meals are just dinners. Some of them come with context. The adobo entry is both a recipe test and a social bet: if you are cooking for your girlfriend's family, maybe start with sincerity and garlic.

  • The rules here are loose. Sometimes there is a polished recipe link. Sometimes the note is the whole point.
  • The tone stays personal. This page works better as a journal with appetite than as a perfect recipe database.
  • The photos stay real. These are phone shots, not food-styling exercises, which is exactly why the page has any charm.
